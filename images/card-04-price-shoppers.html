Mistake #2

You're attracting the wrong people — and it's your ad's fault

The price you put in your ad is like a filter. Too low and you get everyone. The right price and you get real buyers.

🚫 What most contractors do

"Bathrooms starting at $5,000!"
Attracts people with a $5,000 budget who want a $15,000 remodel. They'll ghost you once they hear the real price.
❌ Round numbers feel fake and attract bargain hunters
❌ Price too low = you get people who can't afford you
❌ You end up wasting time on free estimates that go nowhere

✅ What actually works

"Bathroom remodels starting at $9,500"
Only people who can actually afford you will fill out the form. Fewer leads, but they actually book and pay.
✔ Specific numbers feel honest and professional
✔ The right price scares away tire kickers before they waste your time
✔ You spend less time quoting and more time building
💡 Simple rule: The price in your ad should never be more than 35% below your average job. If your average job is $12,000 — don't advertise below $7,500.
